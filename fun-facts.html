Fun Facts About Attack on Titan

Discover fascinating trivia and behind-the-scenes secrets

Series Origins & Creation

  • Hajime Isayama started working on Attack on Titan when he was just 19 years old
  • The series was initially rejected by several publishers before being accepted by Kodansha
  • Isayama based the Titans' appearance on drunk people he encountered at internet cafes
  • The author originally planned for the manga to end much darker, with most characters dying
  • Attack on Titan was inspired by the video game "Muv-Luv Alternative"

Character Secrets

  • Levi was originally not supposed to be a main character but became incredibly popular
  • Eren's name is derived from the Turkish word "Eren" meaning saint or holy person
  • Mikasa is named after the famous Japanese battleship from World War II
  • Armin's character design was based on a young Björk
  • Captain Levi is only 160cm (5'3") tall, making him one of the shortest adult characters
  • Hange Zoe's gender was intentionally left ambiguous by the author

World Building Details

  • The walls are named after three daughters in Norse mythology: Maria, Rose, and Sina
  • The Survey Corps has an 80% casualty rate on expeditions outside the walls
  • The 3D Maneuver Gear uses a fictional substance called "Iceburst Stone" for propulsion
  • Each blade used with the 3D Maneuver Gear is designed to be disposable after dulling on Titan skin
  • The geography inside the walls is based on Germany and medieval European architecture

Production & Anime Facts

  • The anime's first season took 4 years to produce due to the studio's commitment to quality
  • The iconic opening theme "Guren no Yumiya" by Linked Horizon became a massive hit in Japan
  • Over 1,000 people auditioned for the role of Eren Yeager in the Japanese version
  • The anime uses actual German words in the soundtrack for authenticity
  • Studio WIT hand-drew many Titan animations instead of using CGI for more disturbing effects

Record Breaking Statistics

  • Attack on Titan has sold over 110 million copies worldwide
  • It won the Kodansha Manga Award in 2011
  • The series has been translated into more than 20 languages
  • Season 3 Part 2's finale was the highest-rated anime episode on IMDb with a perfect 10/10
  • The final chapter broke Bessatsu Shonen Magazine's sales records

Easter Eggs & Hidden Details

  • Many character names are references to historical figures and places
  • The year the story begins (845) references the fall of the Western Roman Empire
  • Isayama included several foreshadowing clues in early chapters that only made sense years later
  • The author hid his self-portrait in the manga as a background Titan
  • Several scenes in the anime contain hidden messages written in the background
  • The opening sequences contain major spoilers that only become apparent after watching later seasons

Cultural Impact

  • Attack on Titan sparked a global phenomenon and helped popularize anime in Western countries
  • The series has been analyzed in academic papers discussing themes of freedom and oppression
  • Real-world political movements have referenced the show's symbolism
  • Multiple theme parks in Japan have created Attack on Titan attractions
  • The series influenced fashion trends with Survey Corps jacket designs becoming popular streetwear
